• Study the cepstrum for different regions (voiced/unvoiced/silence) of the speech signal using the examples provided.

    • Select one of the example waveforms provided and click on the 'Load' button.

    • Select a short segment (30 ms) of voiced speech using 'Zoom to selection' button and observe the spectrum as well as cepstrum by clicking on corresponding buttons.

    • Perform the experiment for unvoiced (fricatives and stops) and silence regions.

  • The cepstrum shows a peak at around the time lag corresponding to the pitch period for segments of voiced speech.

    • Measure the pitch period of a voiced segment using the cepstrum.

  • Deriving source and filter components from cepstrum

    • Select only the first few samples of the cepstrum using 'Zoom to selection' button of the cepstrum section.

    • Click the 'Liftered Spectrum' button to obtain the resonances of the system component.

    • In the cepstrum, select the samples around the peak corresponding to the pitch period and click the 'Liftered Spectrum' button to obtain the harmonics corresponding to the source component.
